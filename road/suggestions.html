FAQ - Road Emergencies

What should I do if my car breaks down on the road?
If your car breaks down on the road, try to move it to a safe location, such as the shoulder of the road or a nearby parking lot. Turn on your hazard lights to alert other drivers, and if possible, raise the hood of your car to signal that you need assistance. Contact a roadside assistance service or emergency services for help.
What should I do if I get a flat tire while driving?
If you get a flat tire while driving, safely pull over to the side of the road or a designated emergency stopping area. Turn on your hazard lights and apply the parking brake. Use caution when changing the tire, and if you're unable to do so yourself, contact a roadside assistance service for help.
What should I do if I'm involved in a car accident?
If you're involved in a car accident, first check yourself and others for injuries. If anyone is injured, call emergency services immediately. Move your vehicle to a safe location if possible, and exchange information with the other parties involved in the accident. Take photos of the scene and gather witness contact information. Contact your insurance company to report the accident.
What should I do if my car runs out of gas on the road?
If your car runs out of gas on the road, safely pull over to the side of the road or a nearby parking lot. Turn on your hazard lights to alert other drivers. If you have access to a gas can and a nearby gas station, you can attempt to walk to the gas station to purchase fuel. Alternatively, contact a roadside assistance service for fuel delivery.
What should I do if my car battery dies?
If your car battery dies, safely pull over to the side of the road or a parking lot. Turn off any unnecessary electrical devices to conserve battery power. If you have access to jumper cables and another vehicle with a working battery, you can attempt to jump-start your car. Alternatively, contact a roadside assistance service for a jump-start.
